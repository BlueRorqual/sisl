spin_moment¶

sisl.physics.electron.spin_moment(eig_v, S=None)[source]¶

Calculate the spin magnetic moment (also known as spin texture)

This calculation only makes sense for non-colinear calculations.

The returned quantities are given in this order:

  • Total spin magnetic moment
  • Spin magnetic moment along \(x\) direction
  • Spin magnetic moment along \(y\) direction
  • Spin magnetic moment along \(z\) direction

These are calculated using the Pauli matrices \(\boldsymbol\sigma_x\), \(\boldsymbol\sigma_y\) and \(\boldsymbol\sigma_z\):

\[\begin{split}\mathbf{S}_i &= \sum_i \langle \psi_i |\boldsymbol\sigma_z \mathbf S \boldsymbol\sigma_z | \psi_i \rangle \\ \mathbf{S}_i^x(E) &= \sum_i \langle \psi_i | \boldsymbol\sigma_x \mathbf S | \psi_i \rangle \\ \mathbf{S}_i^y(E) &= \sum_i \langle \psi_i | \boldsymbol\sigma_y \mathbf S | \psi_i \rangle \\ \mathbf{S}_i^z(E) &= \sum_i \langle \psi_i | \boldsymbol\sigma_z \mathbf S | \psi_i \rangle\end{split}\]

Note that the total spin magnetic moment, \(\mathbf S\) may be calculated using \(\boldsymbol\sigma_i\boldsymbol\sigma_i\) where \(i\) may be either of \(x\), \(y\) or \(z\) as indicated in the first equation above.

Parameters:

eig_v : array_like

eigenvectors

S : array_like, optional

overlap matrix used in the \(\langle\psi|\mathbf S|\psi\rangle\) calculation. If None the identity matrix is assumed. The overlap matrix should correspond to the system and \(k\) point the eigenvectors have been evaluated at.
Returns:

numpy.ndarray

spin moments per eigenvector with final dimension (4, eig_v.shape[0]).

See also

DOS
total DOS
PDOS
projected DOS

Notes

This routine cannot check whether the input eigenvectors originate from a non-colinear calculation. If a non-polarized eigenvector is passed to this routine, the output will have no physical meaning.
