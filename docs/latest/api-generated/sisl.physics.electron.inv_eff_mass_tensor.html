inv_eff_mass_tensor¶

sisl.physics.electron.inv_eff_mass_tensor(state, ddHk, energy=None, ddSk=None, degenerate=None)[source]¶

Calculate the effective mass tensor for a set of states

These are calculated using the analytic expression (\(\alpha\) corresponding to the Cartesian directions):

\[\mathbf M_i = \hbar^2 \langle \psi_i | \nabla^2_{\mathbf k} \mathbf H(\mathbf k) | \psi_i \rangle^{-1}\]

In case of non-orthogonal basis the equations requires the energy corresponding to the state and the overlap matrix derivative

\[\mathbf M_i = \hbar^2 \langle \psi_i | \nabla^2_{\mathbf k} \mathbf H(\mathbf k) - \epsilon_i\nabla^2_{\mathbf k} \mathbf S(\mathbf k) | \psi_i \rangle^{-1}\]

where \(\psi_i\) are now states in the non-orthogonal basis.

The matrix \(\mathbf M\) is known as the effective mass tensor.

Parameters:

state : array_like

vectors describing the electronic states, 2nd dimension contains the states

ddHk : (3, 3) of array_like

Hamiltonian double derivative with respect to \(\mathbf k\).

energy : array_like, optional

energies of the states. Required for non-orthogonal basis together with dSk.

ddSk : (3, 3) of array_like, optional

\(\nabla^2_k \mathbf S_k\) matrix required for non-orthogonal basis. This and energy must both be provided in a non-orthogonal basis (otherwise the results will be wrong). Same derivative as ddHk

degenerate: list of array_like, optional

a list containing the indices of degenerate states. In that case a subsequent diagonalization is required to decouple them.
Returns:

numpy.ndarray

effective mass of each state in units of electron mass
